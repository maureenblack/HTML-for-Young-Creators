HTML Basics: Let's Build a Webpage about Mount Eloumdem! 🏗️

Understanding HTML: Building Our Mountain Webpage 📝

Just like Mount Eloumdem has different parts - its base, slopes, and peak - websites also have different parts that work together! Let's learn how HTML helps us create a beautiful webpage about our mountain:

What is HTML? 🤔

HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language. Think of it as our toolbox for building webpages:

  • HyperText: Like trails connecting different parts of Mount Eloumdem, hypertext connects different parts of our website
  • Markup: Just as we use signs to mark trails on the mountain, we use HTML to mark different parts of our webpage
  • Language: A set of rules that helps us organize our content, like the rules hikers follow on the mountain

Let's see how HTML works with a simple example about Mount Eloumdem:

<h1>Welcome to Mount Eloumdem</h1>
<p>A beautiful mountain in Yaoundé, Cameroon</p>

The browser knows that "Welcome to Mount Eloumdem" is a big heading, and the description is a paragraph!

HTML Tags: The Building Blocks of Our Mountain Guide 🧱

Basic Page Structure

Just like Mount Eloumdem has its structure, our webpage needs a proper structure too:

<!DOCTYPE html>            👈 Like saying "This is a modern webpage about Mount Eloumdem"
<html>                    👈 The whole mountain (our entire webpage)
    <head>                👈 Planning our mountain tour (webpage information)
        <title>Mount Eloumdem Guide</title>
    </head>
    <body>                👈 The actual mountain tour (visible content)
        Your mountain content goes here!
    </body>
</html>

Text Tags: Describing Our Mountain 📝

Headings: Like Mountain Signposts

Just as Mount Eloumdem has different levels of paths, HTML has different levels of headings:

<h1>Mount Eloumdem</h1>              👈 Main title (the mountain itself)
<h2>About the Mountain</h2>          👈 Major sections (like different areas)
<h3>Flora and Fauna</h3>            👈 Sub-sections (specific features)

Paragraphs: Telling the Mountain's Story

<p>Mount Eloumdem is a beautiful mountain located in Yaoundé, Cameroon. 
Its scenic trails offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.</p>

<p>Visitors can enjoy <strong>hiking</strong> and 
<em>bird watching</em> on the mountain.</p>

Lists: Mountain Activities and Features

We can list things about Mount Eloumdem in two ways:

<ul>                        👈 Features of the mountain
    <li>Beautiful hiking trails</li>
    <li>Diverse wildlife</li>
    <li>Amazing viewpoints</li>
</ul>

<ol>                        👈 Steps to reach the summit
    <li>Start at the base camp</li>
    <li>Follow the marked trail</li>
    <li>Reach the viewpoint</li>
</ol>

Images: Showing Off Our Mountain 🖼️

Let's add some beautiful pictures of Mount Eloumdem:

<img src="mount_eloumdem.jpg" alt="Scenic view of Mount Eloumdem at sunset">

Tip: The alt text helps people understand what the image shows if they can't see it!

Links: Connecting Mountain Information 🔗

Create links to different parts of our mountain guide:

<a href="trails.html">Explore Mount Eloumdem Trails</a>

Trail Markers: Avoiding Common Mistakes ⚠️

Just like following the right trail on Mount Eloumdem, we need to follow the right HTML rules:

  • Always close your tags: 
    ✅ Correct:
<p>Mount Eloumdem is beautiful</p>

❌ Wrong:
<p>Mount Eloumdem is beautiful
  • Use quotes for attributes: 
    ✅ Correct:
<img src="mountain_view.jpg" alt="Mount Eloumdem peak">

❌ Wrong:
<img src=mountain_view.jpg alt=Mount Eloumdem peak>
